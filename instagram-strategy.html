Reference Study

Reference 01

Blvck Paris (@black)

2.3M followers · Following 8 people

Built on Instagram before a single product existed

What they do: Obsessive consistency around a single aesthetic — everything is black, no exceptions. The feed isn't random posts, it's a curated lifestyle world. They post product shots, flat lays, model content, and branded lifestyle imagery all in one colour tone. The grid reads as one unified image, not individual posts.

What we take: The discipline. Every post must feel like it belongs to the same world. No random content. No off-brand moments. The feed is the brand.

Reference 02

Fear of God (@fearofgod)

2M followers · "American Luxury"

Jerry Lorenzo — founder-led, intentional, sparse

What they do: Slow. Deliberate. Every post is editorial — full-body campaign shots, warm neutral backgrounds, minimal text. They don't post daily. Each post commands attention because there isn't noise around it. Captions are short, sometimes just a single word or a date. The product speaks, the founder speaks. Nothing else.

What we take: The pace and the restraint. Less is more. Each post needs to mean something. Full-body model shots, warm tones, minimal copy.

Content Types — Real vs AI

Shoot yourself

Real Content — Highest Priority

This is your edge over every AI-only brand. Real photography from someone who actually knows how to shoot. This is what makes the feed feel premium and trustworthy.

  • Model shots on seamless — warm chalk or graphite background
  • Outdoor worn — morning light, natural environments, Brisbane locations
  • Sample unboxings — hands, packaging, fabric texture close-ups
  • Behind the scenes — factory correspondence, spec sheets, swatches arriving
  • Founder content — Ryan wearing the pieces, no performance needed
AI generated

AI Content — Fill & Consistency

Keeps the feed active and consistent before samples arrive, and supplements real content long-term. Use AI for product imagery, moodboards, and atmospheric posts — never for moments that need to feel human.

  • Product shots on seamless — tees, hoodies, track pants, accessories
  • Flat lay compositions — folded pieces, colour groupings
  • Texture close-ups — fabric grain, stitch detail, waistband close
  • Mood imagery — minimal rooms, morning light scenes, neutral palettes
  • Colour palette posts — swatches, Pantone-style reference cards

Photography Rules — Non-Negotiable

🌡

Warm tones only

Every image must sit in the chalk / graphite / sand palette. No cool blue shadows. No harsh white light. Think morning sun through a window, not a flash-lit studio.

📐

4:5 ratio, always

Instagram's native portrait format. No squares. No landscape. 4:5 on every single post — product, model, mood, everything. Consistency builds the grid.

🤫

No clutter

One subject per frame. Empty backgrounds. Negative space is not wasted space — it's what makes the subject land. Fear of God never clutters a frame.

👤

Full body on model

When using a model, show the full outfit. Cropped shots lose the proportion story. FOG always shows full body — you understand the garment because you see how it sits.

🚫

No logos visible

Still doesn't rely on a logo to communicate quality. Never place a logo front and centre in a photo. The clothing speaks. The brand doesn't need to shout.

✂️

Edit with restraint

Light grading only. Slight warmth, slight desaturation. Never heavily filtered. The Blvck approach — consistent tone across every post — but warmer. Same preset, every image.

Length 1–4 lines maximum. Blvck and FOG both use short captions. Silence communicates confidence. Long captions feel like justification.
Tone Declarative. Never salesy. Never "shop now" energy in the caption itself. State the fact, let it land. The link in bio handles the rest.
Product posts Name + one key spec + one line of meaning. That's it. No price in caption, no discount, no urgency.
Mood posts One sentence or nothing. Sometimes just "Still." as the entire caption. Let the image do the work.
Hashtags 5 max, placed in first comment not the caption. #still #menswear #premiumbasics #slowfashion #foundationcollection — never generic (#fashion, #style, #ootd).
Emoji None. Ever. Blvck uses them occasionally. FOG never does. Still doesn't.

AI Tools for Content Creation

Product imagery

SDXL-Turbo (local, already set up)

Already installed on your M2. Use for product shots on seamless backgrounds, flat lays, texture close-ups. Best for simple single-item shots. Prompts should specify: chalk/graphite background, overhead or front-on, soft diffused lighting, no model.

Model / lifestyle

Midjourney v6 or Flux

For AI model shots and lifestyle scenes — significantly better at human anatomy than SDXL-Turbo. Use prompt style: "still menswear campaign, model wearing [piece], warm chalk seamless, full body, FOG editorial style, soft natural light, 4:5".

Video / Reels

Runway Gen-3 or Kling

Generate short atmospheric video clips — fabric movement, a model walking, morning light through a window. Combine with your own real footage for Reels. AI for b-roll, real for hero content.

Caption & copy

Claude (already here)

Write captions in the Still voice — declarative, short, no emoji, no sales language. Brief: "Write a 2-line caption for a post showing [product]. Still brand voice — confident, minimal, no fluff." Review and approve before posting.

Posting Cadence

Pre-launch 3–4 posts per week. Build the world before the product exists. AI product shots, mood imagery, behind-the-build content. Goal: 1,000 followers before launch day.
Launch week Daily. One post per day for 7 days. Countdown, product reveals, founder story, launch day, post-launch. This is the spike moment.
Post-launch 4–5 posts per week. Mix of real photography (once samples arrive) and AI fills. Never go dark for more than 2 days.
Reels 1–2 per week minimum. Instagram heavily prioritises Reels for reach. Even 15-second atmospheric clips — fabric texture, a fold, morning light — count. This is how you grow.
Stories Daily when possible. Behind the scenes, polls ("chalk or graphite?"), countdown stickers, waitlist link. Stories keep you top of mind between feed posts.
