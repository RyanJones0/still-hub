01 — Hats

The Cap

No embroidered logos. No plastic snapbacks. A hat made from the same standard as everything else — structured where it needs to be, relaxed where it doesn't. Two versions: one for every day, one for everywhere else.

The Six-Panel $85

Unstructured. Cotton twill shell, cotton sweatband. Adjustable with a clean brass buckle at the back. No external branding.

The Wool Cap $105

Structured 6-panel. 80% Merino wool, 20% nylon. Pre-curved peak. Tonal taping on the interior brim. One size, fully adjustable.

The Bucket $95

Medium brim. 100% washed cotton canvas. Clean seams, no grommets, no eyelets. Packable. Holds its shape after packing.

02 — Hoodies

The Hoodie

Most hoodies are made from fleece that goes soft immediately and loses its structure within months. Ours uses structured French terry — 380gsm, woven tight, architectural through the chest and shoulder. Two versions: pullover and zip.

Foundation Hoodie $185

380gsm French terry. Structured pullover silhouette. Set-in sleeve. Clean kangaroo pocket with internal phone divider. Ribbed cuffs and hem. Drawcord with gunmetal tips. No logo — just a small interior label.

Woven in Lyon, France · Made in Portugal
The Zip $205

Same French terry as the pullover. Full-length YKK zip in gunmetal. No hood drawcord (cleaner silhouette). Two zip pockets at hip — no hand warmer pocket. Structured enough to wear over a shirt and under a jacket.

Woven in Lyon, France · Made in Portugal

Long-Sleeve Foundation $125

05 — Tees

The Tee

The most over-made, under-considered garment in any wardrobe. Ours starts with 230gsm Pima — heavier than most brands dare, because weight is what gives a tee presence. Two cuts: foundation (relaxed) and fitted. Both earn the price tag.

Foundation Tee $95

230gsm Pima cotton. Pre-washed for a settled hand-feel. Set-in sleeve. Slightly extended body and hem. Crew neck with 1x1 rib collar. No side seams — tubular knit for a cleaner line. Gets better with every wash.

Woven in Peru · Made in Peru
Fitted Tee $95

Same 230gsm Pima as the Foundation. Shorter body, closer to the torso, narrower sleeve. For when you want the tee to be the point. Not slim-fit — fitted. There's a difference.

Woven in Peru · Made in Peru
Heavyweight Tee $110

280gsm garment-dyed Pima. The tee with presence. Boxy fit, drop shoulder, slightly shortened body. Looks better the more you wear it. Fades intentionally. Garment-dyed for a lived-in starting point.

Woven in Peru · Made in Peru · Garment-dyed in Portugal

Foundation Track Pant $195

380gsm French terry. Matching fabric to the Foundation Hoodie — worn as a set or separated. Tapered leg. Ribbed ankle cuff. Hidden drawcord at waistband. Two side pockets, one zip back pocket. No external branding.

Woven in Lyon, France · Made in Portugal
Tech Track Pant $225

220gsm Japanese technical twill — 4-way stretch, moisture-wicking, crease-resistant. Looks like a tailored pant, performs like an athletic one. Wears to dinner. Wears to the airport. Wears everywhere. Clean ankle — no cuff, no taper break.

Fabric from Osaka, Japan · Made in Portugal

The No-Show $22

75% Egyptian cotton, 20% nylon, 5% elastane. Silicone grip heel — stays put. Seamless toe. Sits below the shoe line on every loafer, sneaker, and low-cut shoe. Sold individually or 3-pack ($60).

Merino Crew $38

70% Merino wool, 25% nylon, 5% elastane. Temperature-regulating. Odour-resistant. For colder months, travel, or anyone who has ever worn a Merino sock and been unable to wear anything else since. Sold individually or 3-pack ($105).
