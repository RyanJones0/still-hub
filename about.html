Why we exist

The space
between.

Still was built around a single frustration: the clothing that performs best rarely looks the part, and the clothing that looks best rarely holds up past 6pm.

We didn't set out to make sportswear. We didn't set out to make formalwear. We set out to make clothing for the space in between — which is where real life actually happens.

The morning coffee that runs long. The walk that becomes a meeting. The dinner that wasn't planned. The day that asks more of you than you expected.

Every piece we make exists because it solved a specific problem in the wardrobe. We don't produce for production's sake. We will never be the brand with the most options. We intend to be the one with the right ones.

We manufacture in Portugal and Peru. We source fabric from Italy, New Zealand, Japan, and France. We publish the origin, weight, and composition of everything we make. Not as a marketing exercise — because we believe you deserve to know exactly what you're wearing.

We don't discount. The price is the price — set to reflect the true cost of making something well, and to be fair to every customer who has ever paid it.

"The most powerful statement is often the most restrained one."

What we're made of.

Ica Valley, Peru

Pima Cotton

Weight 230gsm
Composition 100% Pima
Used in Foundation Tee, Polo

Pima cotton fibres are 45% longer than standard cotton, which means they produce a smoother, softer yarn. The result is a fabric that is resistant to pilling, retains its shape through washing, and becomes more comfortable with every wear. We source exclusively from the Ica Valley, where the combination of desert sun and irrigation produces the finest Pima in the world. The cotton is combed, spun, and woven at 230gsm — heavy enough to have presence, light enough for three-season wear.

Biella, Italy

Merino-TENCEL Blend

Weight 228gsm
Composition 60% Merino / 40% TENCEL
Used in Five-Pocket Trouser

The fabric at the centre of the brand. A blend of superfine Merino wool from New Zealand (17.5 microns — below the threshold for itch) and TENCEL Lyocell from sustainably managed forests. The Merino gives structure, warmth, and natural odour resistance. The TENCEL gives drape, breathability, and the slight sheen that reads as intentional in any room. Woven in Biella — Italy's historic textile capital — to a 228gsm weight that holds its shape through a full day without pressing. Machine washable cold.

Lyon, France

Structured French Terry

Weight 380gsm
Composition 85% Cotton / 15% Nylon
Used in Foundation Hoodie

Most hoodies are made from fleece that goes soft immediately and loses its structure within months. We use a French terry — a looped fabric knitted tight and finished heavy at 380gsm — that is soft but architectural. It holds its shape across the chest and shoulder in a way fleece cannot. The 15% nylon gives it resilience. The cotton gives it warmth. Lyon has produced this fabric construction since the 17th century. Ours is woven to our specification.

South Island, New Zealand

Superfine Merino

Micron 17.5µ
Composition 100% Merino
Used in Half-Zip, Crewneck

At 17.5 microns, this Merino sits at the boundary between standard superfine and ultra-fine. Below 18.5 microns, fibres stop feeling rough against bare skin — the sensation most people associate with wool is gone entirely. What remains is warmth without weight, temperature regulation that synthetic fabrics cannot match, and a natural odour resistance that means you can wear it three days without washing. Sourced from a single station on New Zealand's South Island where the flock grazes at altitude year-round.

Where we make it

Two countries.
Every piece by hand.

Portugal — Porto

Knitwear & Trousers

Our Portuguese factory has operated in Porto since 1987. They run one shift. Every piece is inspected by hand before it leaves the floor. We visited before we placed our first order and we visit every season. The people who make our clothes are paid above the national living wage and work under a collective agreement that has been in place for 22 years.

Peru — Lima

Cotton Basics

Peru is the source of the finest Pima cotton in the world and has been for a century. Our Lima factory specialises exclusively in Pima — they do not work with inferior cotton because they don't need to. They cut and sew our Foundation Tee, Long-Sleeve, and Polo. Lead time is 14 weeks. We don't rush it.
